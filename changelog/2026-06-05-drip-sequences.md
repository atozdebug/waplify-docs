Drip Sequences are here — set up a series of WhatsApp messages once, and Waplify follows up with each contact automatically over days or weeks.

<!-- truncate -->

## What you can do

A **drip sequence** sends up to **7 messages** to each contact, one at a time, with a delay you choose between each. The first message goes out the moment a contact is enrolled; the follow-ups send on schedule after that — perfect for welcome series, onboarding, lead and cart recovery, and re-engagement.

You'll find it under **Sequences** in the sidebar. Full walkthrough: [What is a Drip Sequence?](/docs/sequences/what-are-sequences).

## Build it in three steps

The new builder walks you through it:

- **Details & audience** — name your sequence and pick who receives it (contacts, groups, pasted numbers, or all contacts with a filter), opted-in only by default
- **Messages** — add your approved templates, personalize them with contact fields and fallbacks, attach image/video/document media, and set the delay before each step
- **Launch** — review the timeline, then **Run now** or **Schedule for later** in any timezone

See [Creating a Sequence](/docs/sequences/creating-a-sequence).

## Control the timing and delivery

- **Send window** — choose the days and hours your follow-ups are allowed to go out, in your workspace timezone
- **Wait for delivery** — optionally hold each message until WhatsApp confirms the previous one arrived
- **Stop on reply** — when a customer replies, hand the conversation to your team in the inbox and end their journey

More in [Send Window & Delivery Settings](/docs/sequences/send-window-and-delivery).

## See exactly how it's performing

Every sequence has a full analytics page: enrolled, delivered, read, replied, clicked, and failed rates; a per-message breakdown with real failure reasons; an activity-over-time chart; and a searchable log of every contact's journey that you can export to CSV.

Details in [Sequence Analytics](/docs/sequences/sequence-analytics).

## Manage them with confidence

Run a sequence with one click from the list, **pause and resume** any time (the schedule shifts so nobody gets a burst of catch-up messages), edit drafts and paused sequences, and remove individual contacts. See [Managing Sequences](/docs/sequences/managing-sequences).
