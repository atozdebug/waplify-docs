Your messages just got richer: **a different image or video on every carousel card**, **countdown offer buttons** that create urgency, and **photo and document uploads** inside WhatsApp Forms.

<!-- truncate -->

## A different image on every carousel card

Carousel templates let you show several products in one swipeable message. Now each card can carry **its own image or video** instead of sharing one picture across the whole set.

- Upload media **per card** while building the template — card one shows the sofa, card two shows the dining table, card three shows the bed.
- Works everywhere carousels do: [campaigns](/docs/campaigns/creating-campaigns), [sequences](/docs/sequences/creating-a-sequence), and chatbot flows.
- The **preview updates as you go**, so you see exactly what the customer will swipe through before you send it.

This is the closest thing WhatsApp has to a shop window — use it for new arrivals, a menu, a property list, or a service lineup.

See [Carousel Templates](/docs/templates/carousel-templates) to build one.

## Limited-time offer buttons

You can now send templates with a **limited-time offer** — WhatsApp shows a live countdown on the message itself, and the offer button disappears when the timer runs out.

Paired with **dynamic buttons**, whose link changes per contact, you can send one campaign where every customer gets their own coupon code or personalised checkout link, wrapped in a deadline they can actually see ticking.

Genuine urgency, without you having to chase anyone.

## Ask for photos and documents in a form

[WhatsApp Forms](/docs/flows-v2/asking-questions-and-saving-answers) can now collect files, not just typed answers.

- Add a **Photo** question — customers attach pictures straight from their camera or gallery.
- Add a **Document** question — for PDFs, receipts, ID copies, prescriptions, or quotes.
- Set **how many files** they can attach and the **maximum size**, so nobody sends you forty photos by accident.

This unlocks the jobs businesses were doing by hand before: damage photos for a claim, a measurement photo before a quote, proof of payment, a signed document, a prescription upload.

## See what they sent, right in the submissions table

Uploaded files show up in your **form submissions** alongside the typed answers. Photos appear as thumbnails you can open full-size, and documents can be downloaded in one click — no hunting through the chat to find the attachment.

## Cleaner template variables

One small fix with a big payoff: the template editor now **stops you mixing numbered variables (`{{1}}`) with named ones (`{{first_name}}`) in the same template**. That combination was a common reason for templates being rejected by Meta or sending with blank gaps. Now it's caught while you're writing, not after approval fails.

See [Template Variables & Media](/docs/templates/template-variables-and-media) for the full picture.
